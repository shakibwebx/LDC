The Leading Rhinophyma Treatment Clinic in London

Rhinophyma is a skin condition that causes thickening and enlargement of the nose, often resulting in a bulbous and uneven appearance. It tends to occur in more advanced cases of rosacea and can have a significant impact on both physical appearance and self-confidence.

At the London Dermatology Centre, we offer a comprehensive range of effective rhinophyma treatments tailored to each patient. Whether you're experiencing early signs or more advanced symptoms, our team of experienced dermatologists will work with you to find the most suitable treatment plan for your skin.

What Is Rhinophyma?

Rhinophyma is a progressive condition that leads to the thickening of the skin on the nose due to an overgrowth of sebaceous (oil) glands and connective tissue. It typically develops over time and is more common in men than women, especially in those with a long-standing history of rosacea.

Common signs include:

  • Redness and swelling of the nose
  • Thickened, bumpy skin
  • Enlarged pores and prominent oil glands
  • Visible blood vessels (telangiectasia)

The condition is not caused by alcohol, despite popular misconceptions, although alcohol may exacerbate the symptoms in some people.

Why Seek Treatment?

Rhinophyma can cause more than just cosmetic concerns. In advanced cases, the excessive tissue can interfere with nasal airflow, and the irregular skin surface can be prone to infections or irritation. More often, however, patients seek treatment due to the psychological and emotional impact it can have on social interactions and self-esteem.

Early intervention can prevent progression and lead to better long-term outcomes.

Treatment Options at the London Dermatology Centre

We offer a range of rhinophyma treatment options depending on the severity of the condition and the individual needs of the patient. These include:

Medical Management

In mild cases or early stages, we may recommend medications to control underlying rosacea and reduce inflammation. These can include:

  • Topical treatments such as metronidazole or azelaic acid
  • Oral antibiotics to reduce inflammation
  • Isotretinoin in selected cases to reduce sebaceous gland activity

Laser Treatment

Laser therapy is often highly effective for reducing excess tissue and improving the appearance of the nose. We offer several laser options, including:

  • CO2 Laser Resurfacing: Precisely removes layers of thickened skin while promoting collagen remodelling.
  • Erbium:YAG Laser: A gentler option that targets superficial tissue. Laser treatment is typically performed under local anaesthetic with minimal downtime.

Surgical Debulking

For more severe cases, surgical reshaping may be required to remove bulky tissue and restore the natural contours of the nose. This is done with meticulous care to preserve function and achieve a more balanced aesthetic outcome. Techniques may involve:

  • Scalpel excision
  • Electrosurgery
  • Dermabrasion in combination with laser

Our consultant dermatologists and surgeons work together to ensure optimal cosmetic and medical results.

Frequently Asked Questions

What causes rhinophyma?

Rhinophyma is most commonly linked to long-standing rosacea, particularly the subtype known as phymatous rosacea. While the exact cause isn't fully understood, it involves inflammation and overgrowth of sebaceous glands in the skin. Factors such as genetics, sun exposure, and untreated rosacea may contribute to its development.

Is rhinophyma related to alcohol consumption?

No, rhinophyma is not caused by alcohol. While alcohol can trigger facial flushing and worsen rosacea symptoms in some individuals, the belief that alcohol directly causes rhinophyma is a myth. The condition can occur in people who do not drink alcohol at all.

Can rhinophyma go away on its own?

Rhinophyma is a progressive condition and does not typically resolve without treatment. In fact, it can gradually worsen over time if left untreated. Early medical intervention can help manage symptoms and slow progression, while advanced cases may require laser or surgical treatment.

How is rhinophyma diagnosed?

Rhinophyma is diagnosed clinically by a dermatologist based on the characteristic appearance of the skin, particularly the thickening and irregularity of the nose. In some cases, further evaluation may be done to rule out other skin conditions, but a biopsy is rarely needed.

What treatment is best for me?

The most suitable treatment depends on how advanced your rhinophyma is and whether active inflammation is present. Our dermatologists will assess your condition during a consultation and recommend a personalised treatment plan, which may include medication, laser therapy, or surgical reshaping.

Is treatment painful?

Medical and topical treatments are generally well tolerated. Laser and surgical procedures are performed under local anaesthetic, so you shouldn’t feel pain during the procedure itself. Some mild discomfort or sensitivity can be expected during the healing process, but this is typically manageable with aftercare advice we provide.

How long is the recovery time after laser or surgery?

Recovery time varies depending on the treatment used. Most patients resume normal activities within a few days after laser treatment, though healing can take up to two weeks. Surgical procedures may require slightly longer downtime, and we'll provide detailed post-treatment instructions to help your recovery go smoothly.

Will I need more than one treatment?

This depends on the severity of your rhinophyma and the treatment method used. Some patients achieve excellent results after one session, especially with surgery or ablative laser. Others may benefit from a staged approach or additional follow-ups for optimal results.

Can rhinophyma return after treatment?

Rhinophyma can recur if the underlying rosacea is not managed properly. That’s why we often recommend ongoing rosacea treatment and regular dermatology reviews to help maintain your results and prevent further progression.
