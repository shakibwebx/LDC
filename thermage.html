What happens to collagen as we age?

Collagen is the most abundant protein in the human body and is responsible for the skin's elasticity. Over time, the skin loses its volume as the amount of collagen diminishes. This thinning of areas such as the cheeks, lips, and around the mouth occurs due to ageing, partly because of the loss of subcutaneous (under the skin) fat. As a result, facial muscles work closer to the skin surface, making facial lines appear more defined and noticeable. This is a perfectly normal part of the ageing process, but these lines and wrinkles can be unwelcome and cause a lack of confidence in individuals. If you're looking for an effective non-surgical solution, Thermage could be the ideal choice for you to help rejuvenate your skin and restore your confidence.

How does Thermage work?

Thermage works by stimulating the body's natural renewal of collagen, creating youthful-looking results. Thermage heats the deep, collagen-dense layers of the skin through radiofrequency technology. The heat applied separates water molecules from collagen in the skin, causing an immediate contraction that results in skin tightening. Additionally, the heat modifies the existing collagen and increases the production of new collagen, which cumulatively improves the texture of the skin. New collagen growth from treatment tightens the skin over time. This secondary healing response, which remodels collagen, can lead to long-lasting results of up to 2 years. The radiofrequency waves that heat the skin treat the outer skin and then extend to the inner layers. Treatment involves a radiofrequency wand that targets specific areas. What parts of the body can Thermage treat?

Thermage is an effective skin tightening treatment that can be used on various parts of the body, including the face, neck, décolletage (women’s low neckline), and body. This versatile treatment can improve the appearance of loose jowls (lower cheeks) and sagging skin on the face, which often contribute to a tired look. By smoothing out wrinkles and remodeling collagen, Thermage rejuvenates the skin and restores a more youthful appearance.

Eyes are one of the most obvious signs of aging, particularly hooded eyes, where excess skin folds from the brow. Thermage is suitable for treating both the upper and lower eyelids, resulting in a subtly more energized appearance. Thermage treatment is not limited to the face and eyes. It is also optimal for enhancing and contouring the appearance of crepey skin on the body. Crepey skin, which is thin, finely wrinkled, and sometimes saggy, cannot usually be reversed with home remedies. Consulting a dermatologist can help determine the best treatment, and Thermage is often a recommended option. Thermage can treat both smaller and larger areas of the body, providing comprehensive skin tightening and rejuvenation.

Thermage can help reduce the appearance of:

  • Sagging skin, including under the chin
  • Lack of jawline definition
  • Loose jowls
  • Wrinkles
  • Mouth lines
  • Forehead lines
  • Crepey skin on abdomen, legs, and arm

What are the benefits of Thermage?

Thermage is a non-invasive procedure, meaning no cutting and no needles. This is ideal for patients who have a fear of needles or are generally squeamish regarding invasive treatments. Results include skin tightening and contouring, and the treatment requires no downtime. Skin improvements are noticeable after a single treatment. The process of collagen stimulation creates gradual, natural-looking results, leaving patients with youthful-looking skin. Thermage is suitable for all skin types and can be used on most areas of the skin, whether on the face or body. It is an overall safe and effective treatment for skin tightening.

What happens during the Thermage procedure?

Our dermatology clinic in London has been treating patients for nearly two decades, so you can be assured you are in safe hands. Since Thermage is a non-surgical treatment, your preparation before coming to the clinic is minimal. You will be asked to arrive with a clean face, free of make-up or skin products. It is also recommended to avoid facial peels and chemical treatments beforehand. Your dermatologist or aesthetic practitioner at our Thermage clinic will ensure you thoroughly understand what to expect during the procedure. Your practitioner will perform the treatment using a hand-held device that delivers radiofrequency energy into the dermal tissues of the targeted area. The procedure is quick, lasting between 30 to 90 minutes depending on the treatment area.

This short treatment time is one of the reasons why Thermage is extremely appealing—despite the brief session, the results are long-lasting. Furthermore, Thermage treatment is designed with patient comfort in mind. Integrated cooling is used, and the radiofrequency is pulsed. The tip of the Thermage device vibrates, facilitating a more comfortable experience. You will feel a short-lasting heating sensation. Who is a suitable candidate for Thermage?

Thermage is often described as a non-surgical facelift, making it an excellent option for individuals who are unable to undergo surgery or prefer non-invasive treatments due to phobias or personal reservations. The treatment is suitable for both men and women, especially those with mild to moderate skin laxity that does not require an intense facelift achievable only through surgery. Thermage can be used on most areas of the face and body, making it ideal for patients seeking to expand their treatment options—whether it’s softening fine lines around the eyes or tightening skin on the thighs that remains stubborn despite regular exercise. To determine if Thermage is the optimal treatment for your specific skin needs, you should consult a dermatologist at our clinic in London. They can help you decide if you are a suitable candidate for Thermage therapy.

How long do results of Thermage last?

After a single Thermage treatment, results can last up to 16 months, with the most noticeable improvements occurring between 3-6 months as the body continues to produce new collagen. The longevity of the results can vary depending on the area treated and individual skin characteristics. Your dermatologist at the clinic may recommend incorporating Thermage into a yearly skin rejuvenation maintenance program to sustain the youthful and firm appearance of your skin. Additionally, factors such as lifestyle, skincare regimen, and sun protection practices can influence the duration of the results.

What happens after the Thermage procedure?

Downtime after the Thermage procedure is minimal, often lasting only a few hours if needed. Most patients can resume their daily activities immediately following the treatment. Side effects are rare; however, some patients may experience temporary redness or slight swelling, which typically subsides within 24 hours. One of the significant advantages of Thermage is that it requires no extensive aftercare. Maintaining a regular skincare routine, including the use of sunscreen, is recommended to protect and enhance the results. Regular skin maintenance as part of your basic skincare regimen will help sustain the youthful and firm appearance achieved through Thermage.
