Laser Hair Removal in London

Laser hair removal is a safe and effective treatment for reducing unwanted hair. It also rejuvenates the skin and treats conditions such as rosacea, broken thread veins, and spider naevi. The laser treatment uses a focused beam of light to target pigments in the hair follicles. Hair growth is reduced through a series of treatments using controlled light impulses, known as photo depilation. The light is absorbed by the pigment of the hair, causing the hair follicle to heat up and be destroyed without damaging the surrounding tissue. By removing the hair follicles, hair growth is significantly reduced. We use a state-of-the-art, high-energy IPL (Intense Pulsed Light) laser system for hair removal. Which areas are suitable for IPL laser hair removal?

The IPL laser hair removal machine is versatile and can treat most areas of the body in both women and men, including the face. Common treatment areas include the legs, arms, underarms, back, chest, and bikini line. For facial treatments, areas such as the upper lip, chin, and sideburns are often targeted. If you are interested in hair removal in a specific area, please let your aesthetic practitioner know, and they will be able to advise you on the most appropriate method. One of the advantages of IPL laser hair removal is its efficiency. The IPL treatment head can cover a larger area of skin per light pulse compared to other laser hair treatments. This means that the treatment sessions are not only faster but also more cost-effective, allowing you to achieve smooth, hair-free skin in less time and with fewer sessions.

How does laser hair removal work?

Laser hair removal works by targeting the melanin in the hair follicles using a specific range of light wavelengths. When the treatment head delivers these wavelengths to the treated area, the energy penetrates just below the skin’s surface. The melanin, which is the brown pigment found in hairs, skin, and the iris of the eye, absorbs this energy. As the light is absorbed by the melanin in the hair follicle, it causes the follicle to heat up. This heat effectively destroys the hair follicle without causing any damage to the surrounding tissue. By targeting and removing the hair follicles, hair growth is significantly reduced over time. This process is both precise and effective, making it possible to treat various areas of the body, from larger areas like the legs and back to more delicate areas like the face and bikini line. Multiple treatment sessions are typically required to achieve optimal results, as hair grows in cycles and not all follicles are active at the same time. With each session, more hair follicles are targeted and destroyed, leading to progressively smoother and hair-free skin. Get in touch with our laser hair removal clinic in London now to learn more.

What results will I see and when will I see them?

The number of laser hair removal treatments you need depends on your skin type, hair type, and desired results. The high energy levels of our laser reduce the number of treatments required to achieve hair reduction, making the process more cost-effective compared to extended treatments required by lower energy lasers. We recommend a series of 4 to 6 treatments to see significant results. Each treatment should be carried out every 4 to 6 weeks. The duration of each treatment session ranges from 10 to 20 minutes, depending on the size of the area being treated.

Is laser hair removal safe for my skin?

When carried out by a trained professional, laser hair removal treatment is very safe. As with all procedures, there is a small risk of side effects, but these are rare. Side effects can include slight redness and swelling of the treated area, blistering or bruising, and hyper or hypopigmentation. Any redness or swelling from the IPL treatment generally disappears within a few hours, and the remaining side effects are very rare. At our dermatology clinic in London, our dermatologists and aesthetic practitioners will conduct a thorough assessment and perform a patch test before any treatment to minimise these risks. They will ensure that the treatment is tailored to your specific skin type and condition, providing a safe and effective experience. If you have any concerns, our team will be more than happy to address them and ensure your comfort and safety throughout the process.

What can I expect on the day of my laser hair removal treatment?

On the day of your procedure, your aesthetic practitioner will review everything with you and answer any questions you may have. Then, you’ll lie back and relax on one of our treatment beds while the practitioner applies a cooling gel to the area to be treated. You’ll be asked to wear goggles to protect your eyes from the light of the IPL laser hair removal machine, and then your treatment will begin. The aesthetic practitioner will pass the laser head over the treatment area. You may feel some slight discomfort during the procedure, often described as a pinging sensation.

Is laser hair removal painful?

During the procedure, you may experience a very slight pinging sensation as the IPL laser treatment head is passed over your skin. Patients often describe this sensation as feeling like an elastic band being flicked onto the skin. Before your treatment, you will undergo a patch test with one of our aesthetic practitioners. This ensures that you do not experience any adverse reactions and verifies that your skin type is suitable for laser hair removal treatment. You will also be able to experience what the laser hair removal treatment feels like during this patch test. Laser hair removal treatment is not painful and does not require any local anaesthetic beforehand. Our dermatology clinic is dedicated to providing pain-free treatment, so please speak to us if you have any concerns regarding this.

How to care for your skin after your laser hair removal treatment:

For the best results, we advise you to follow these recommendations. Ensure the treated area is well moisturised with frequent applications of a non-perfumed topical moisturiser, ideally containing an SPF of 25 or greater. Avoid irritating the treated area with harsh chemicals such as acne medications or face scrubs for 1 to 2 days after your treatment. Makeup may be applied immediately after treatment. Gently wash the treated area with mild soap and water. If the area is red or irritated, lightly pat it dry with a towel instead of rubbing it. Minimise sun exposure to prevent further damage, and avoid tanning beds throughout the course of treatment. Apply a sunscreen with an SPF of 25 or greater whenever the area is exposed to the sun.

If you develop a tan within the proposed treatment area, delay further treatments for 2 weeks or until the tan has significantly faded. Avoid excessive heat or friction to the treated area, such as heavy exercise or saunas. Provided there is no persistent redness, blistering, or crusting in the treatment area, you may resume all normal activities.
