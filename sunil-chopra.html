A Leading Dermatologist with Over 30 Years’ Expertise

Dr Sunil Chopra BSc MBBS FRCP is one of London’s most experienced consultant dermatologists and the Clinical Director of the London Dermatology Centre. With more than three decades in clinical practice, research, and innovation, he has helped thousands of patients manage skin concerns ranging from acne, rosacea, and pigmentation problems to complex skin cancers.

Training and Medical Background

Dr Chopra qualified from University College London (UCL) in 1990 with degrees in Medicine (MBBS) and Immunology (BSc). During his early career he completed hospital rotations across several major London teaching hospitals, gaining wide exposure to dermatology, general medicine, and chest medicine.

His postgraduate medical training included placements at University College London Hospitals, Chase Farm Hospital, and a year in respiratory and intensive care medicine at Addenbrooke’s Hospital in Cambridge.

He later specialised in dermatology, training at prestigious centres such as Bart’s and The London Hospitals, Hammersmith and Ealing Hospitals, and UCL Hospitals NHS Trust. His academic curiosity led him to spend two years conducting full-time laboratory research into the neurovascular supply of the skin, developing new immunohistochemical techniques that deepened medical understanding of conditions such as Raynaud’s phenomenon.

Leadership and Clinical Practice

As Clinical Director of the London Dermatology Centre, Dr Chopra oversees all aspects of patient care, clinical governance, and consultant development. Under his direction, the clinic has become one of London’s best-known private dermatology centres, renowned for its personalised approach and use of evidence-based treatments.

He continues to consult on a wide range of skin diseases, with particular interest in:

Skin cancer diagnosis and treatment

including basal cell carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, and actinic keratoses.

Pigmentation disorders

such as melasma and post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation.

Rosacea and acne scarring

integrating medical and laser-based therapies for long-term results.

Scalp disorders

including chronic hair loss, seborrhoeic dermatitis, and inflammatory scalp conditions.

Dr Chopra is widely known for taking time to understand each patient’s lifestyle, health history, and aesthetic goals before recommending a tailored plan that blends medical science with practical, achievable care.

Research and Innovation

Beyond his clinical practice, Dr Chopra has devoted much of his career to research into drug delivery systems for skin disease. He is the inventor of Bleosome™, a patented liposomal formulation of Bleomycin Sulphate designed to deliver chemotherapy directly to skin cancers and pre-cancerous lesions such as actinic keratosis.

This innovation was recognised in 2024 when he received a competitive grant from Innovate UK to further study Bleosome’s safety and effectiveness. His company, SPS Healthcare, now holds several international patents in this area and continues to explore nano-liposomal and topical delivery methods for dermatological treatments.

His pioneering research has appeared in journals including Cancers, Equine Veterinary Education, and the British Journal of Dermatology, with collaborations involving professors at Edinburgh University, Liverpool University, and The Veterinary Journal.

Dr Chopra’s current projects focus on:

  • Topical nanomedicine for non-melanoma skin cancers
  • New formulations for alopecia and scalp disorders (granted patents in the UK, USA and Europe 2021–2025)
  • The use of liposomal bleomycin in equine and human dermatology
  • Combining phototherapy and topical agents for vitiligo

Academic Roles and Teaching

Education has always been central to Dr Chopra’s work. He served as Honorary Senior Lecturer at University College London Medical School from 2008 to 2013, lecturing medical students, chiropodists, and postgraduate trainees preparing for the MRCP exam.

He regularly organises and delivers Continuing Medical Education (CME) events for general practitioners at the London Dermatology Centre, helping GPs stay up-to-date on the latest advances in skin cancer detection, acne management, and ethnic-skin care.

His earlier teaching appointments include posts at Bart’s and The London Medical School, Barking and Dagenham Hospital, and The London Foot Hospital, where he also served as an examiner.

Publications and Academic Contributions

Dr Chopra has authored or co-authored numerous peer-reviewed papers and book chapters on dermatology and pharmacology. Selected highlights include:

“Effective penetration of a liposomal formulation of Bleomycin through ex-vivo skin explants” (Cancers, 2022).

“Treatment of rosacea with intense pulsed light: significant improvement and long-lasting results” (British Journal of Dermatology, 2008).

“Phenotypic and clinical differences between Caucasian and South Asian patients with psoriatic arthritis” (Clinical Rheumatology, 2013).

“A novel high-dose nanosomal tacrolimus as monotherapy for refractory facial vitiligo” (JAAD, 2014).

Author of chapters in Manson’s Textbook of Infectious Diseases (21st & 22nd editions) and Treatment of Cancer (4th Edition) on non-melanoma skin cancer and granulomatous disorders.

His work has been presented internationally at conferences such as the European Academy of Dermatology and Venereology, the American Academy of Dermatology, and the Royal Society of Medicine.

World-Renowned Expertise in Vitiligo Treatment

Dr Sunil Chopra is internationally recognised as a leading expert in vitiligo, a complex pigmentary disorder that causes patches of skin to lose their natural colour. Over the course of his career, he has treated thousands of vitiligo patients from the UK and abroad, earning a reputation for combining scientific precision with a compassionate, patient-centred approach.

Vitiligo is often misunderstood and can have a profound psychological impact. Dr Chopra’s approach begins with a detailed assessment of the condition’s pattern, progression, and potential triggers — including autoimmune, genetic, and oxidative stress factors. From there, he develops tailored treatment plans that may integrate medical, phototherapeutic, and regenerative therapies.

His clinic was among the first in London to offer narrowband UVB phototherapy in combination with topical immunomodulators such as Tacrolimus, significantly improving success rates in facial and segmental vitiligo. His pioneering work on high-dose nanosomal tacrolimus as a monotherapy for refractory facial vitiligo — published in the Journal of the American Academy of Dermatology (2014) — positioned him at the forefront of global vitiligo research.

Dr Chopra’s research also explores the molecular mechanisms of melanocyte regeneration, contributing to new insights into how skin pigment can be restored more predictably and safely. His studies have been presented at major international meetings including the European Academy of Dermatology and Venereology and the American Academy of Dermatology, influencing treatment protocols adopted worldwide.

At the London Dermatology Centre, Dr Chopra continues to advance vitiligo management through a combination of state-of-the-art phototherapy, precision topical formulations, and long-term follow-up strategies designed to minimise relapse. His ongoing clinical focus is on improving treatment response in darker skin tones and reducing post-treatment contrast — areas where his expertise is sought internationally.

For patients, this means access to one of the most comprehensive and evidence-based vitiligo treatment programmes in the UK, led by a dermatologist who not only understands the biology of pigment disorders but also the emotional and social challenges they create.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I make a booking?

The London Dermatology Centre now features an online reservation system, simplifying the appointment scheduling process. To book an appointment with Dr Sunil Chopra, simply click the booking tab at the top right corner of our website to select a suitable time and date. Alternatively, you can contact us at 0207 467 3720 to arrange your appointment over the phone.

Can my GP refer me to you?

Yes, your GP can directly refer you to our dermatology clinic, which might reduce the waiting time to see one of our consultant dermatologists. We will keep your GP updated on your treatment progress after each visit.

Do you accept health insurance?

Many of our patients use their personal health insurance for treatments at our clinic. If you are unsure about your insurance coverage, please contact your insurer directly. Our team is prepared to assist with liaising with your insurer and providing any required documents.

Do you have wheelchair access?

We provide parking spaces directly outside our doors on Wimpole Street and offer a portable wheelchair ramp that can be set up before your arrival. Please inform our team if you require wheelchair access for your appointment so that we can make the necessary arrangements.

Are evening and weekend appointments available?

Our clinic offers appointments during evenings and on Saturdays. If these times suit you better, please contact our reception who will assist you further. Please note, we are typically closed on Sundays, but this can vary.

Will you provide medication should I need them?

Our clinic includes a pharmacy on-site, enabling immediate access to both oral and topical medications. This facility is particularly beneficial for explaining usage instructions and allows you to begin your treatment promptly.

What is your cancellation policy?

As our consultants’ time is in high demand, we ask that you provide us with at least 24 hours' notice if you need to cancel or reschedule your appointment. A £50 holding fee is required to secure your booking, which will be credited towards your treatment if you proceed. Failure to provide timely cancellation may result in the loss of this fee. Consultations usually last about 30 minutes.

Can I choose which dermatologist to see?

You are welcome to select any of our dermatologists or nurses for your consultation, according to your preference. For first-time visitors, we recommend the dermatologist who best aligns with your specific dermatological needs. Our clinic offers both female and male doctors to address any sensitive health concerns.

Opening Hours Notice

The London Dermatology Centre remains open for face-to-face appointments. The safety of our patients and staff is our number one priority.
