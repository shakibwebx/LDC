PDO Thread Lift in London

PDO or polydioxanone threads are innovative threads designed to lift, tighten, and rejuvenate sagging skin. The PDO lift is a non-surgical facelift alternative, utilising these specialised threads to lift and tighten skin around the face and neck while stimulating the network of collagen fibres within the skin. By carefully placing these threads, a more youthful appearance is achieved without the need for surgery or extensive downtime. The results from a PDO thread lift typically last longer than those from dermal fillers and Botox. If you are considering a PDO thread lift in London, get in touch with our dermatology clinic now to arrange a consultation with one of our experienced skin doctors.

This minimally invasive thread lift procedure does not require general anaesthetic and has minimal downtime. PDO threads are made from the same material as dissolvable sutures used in wound closure, but instead of dissolving in two weeks, they take around six to nine months. The PDO thread lift procedure takes approximately 30 minutes, earning it nicknames like the "lunch hour lift," the "half hour facelift," and the "one stitch facelift." After applying local anaesthetic, the threads are inserted into the deeper layers of the skin, lifting and tightening it. This treatment is safe, and most patients can return to work the same day with minimal discomfort. Besides lifting, PDO threads can also stimulate collagen production, making them an excellent complement to other rejuvenating treatments.

How Does PDO Thread Lift Work?

During the procedure, a fine cannula, a specialised needle, is utilised to insert the PDO thread into the skin. The number of threads used varies based on the desired outcome, ranging from a single thread to approximately 50 threads for comprehensive treatment of the face and neck. The fine threads are meticulously guided through the skin and positioned along pre-marked contours. Once the thread is correctly placed, the cannula is removed, leaving the thread securely in place. These threads have tiny barbs or anchors that lock into the skin, holding it in its new, lifted position. Any excess thread is trimmed once the desired lift is achieved.

Immediately after the procedure, the lifting effects of the threads are visible. About a month post-treatment, the rejuvenating effects become more noticeable. New collagen begins to form around the threads, providing support to the skin and enhancing its texture. Neovascularisation, or the formation of new blood vessels, improves microcirculation in the skin, further contributing to a youthful appearance by reducing fine lines over the months following the procedure. Additionally, the threads cause the fatty tissue in the face to contract, resulting in a slimmer, tighter, and firmer appearance. Does THE PDO Thread Lift Treatment Hurt?

Before the PDO thread lift treatment begins, you will receive a local anaesthetic. This numbs the area to be treated, significantly reducing any potential discomfort. The procedure itself is generally well-tolerated, with most patients reporting little to no pain. The local anaesthetic ensures that you remain comfortable throughout the treatment. The fine cannula used to introduce the threads is designed to minimise trauma to the skin, further contributing to a pain-free experience. Some patients may feel a slight tugging or pressure, but this is typically mild and brief. Overall, the procedure is designed to be as comfortable as possible, allowing you to achieve rejuvenated, lifted skin with minimal discomfort. If you have any concerns about pain or discomfort, our experienced dermatologists are here to discuss your worries and ensure a smooth, reassuring treatment process.

What Areas of the Face Can a PDO Thread Lift Treat?

A PDO thread lift effectively rejuvenates and lifts various facial areas, similar to a traditional facelift. It stimulates collagen production, enhancing skin texture and firmness. This treatment is particularly effective for contouring and adding definition to key facial regions. The PDO thread lift can redefine the jawline, reduce sagging, and create a more sculpted appearance. It lifts and contours the cheeks, restoring volume and a natural, youthful look. By lifting the eyebrows, the procedure opens up the eye area, giving a more refreshed appearance. It also tightens and firms sagging neck skin, resulting in a smoother, more defined neck area. How Long Do the Results of a PDO Thread Lift Last?

You should see an immediate enhancement in your appearance following a PDO thread lift, with further improvements developing over several months as your body generates new collagen. This gradual process helps to rejuvenate the skin and enhance the initial lift. Typically, the PDO threads dissolve around nine months after the procedure. However, the positive effects and improvements can continue for several months beyond this period. The longevity of the results varies depending on individual factors such as age and the extent of sagging corrected during the treatment. Each person's results may differ, but a top-up treatment with additional threads can be performed six to nine months after the initial procedure. This can extend the benefits of the thread lift for up to three years.

What Material Are PDO Threads Made From?

The threads used in a PDO thread lift are composed of Polydioxanone (PDO), a polymer that has a long history of use in surgical sutures. This material is renowned for its strength, biocompatibility, and safety. Over time, PDO threads naturally break down and are absorbed by the body, making them a well-tolerated option for non-surgical facial lifting and rejuvenation. Their ability to gradually dissolve while stimulating collagen production ensures both immediate and long-lasting results, enhancing skin structure and appearance.

Is a PDO Thread Lift Safe?

When performed by a skilled and experienced practitioner, a PDO thread lift is a very safe procedure. It has a better safety profile compared to fillers and is significantly safer than a surgical facelift. The treatment may cause minor side effects such as bruising, swelling, tenderness, numbness, and slight asymmetry. However, these effects typically resolve within a few days. Complications are uncommon, especially when the procedure is carried out by a trained professional. Before proceeding with the treatment, we will thoroughly discuss all the benefits and potential risks with you to ensure you make an informed decision.

Can Anyone Have a PDO Thread Lift?

A PDO thread lift is most suitable for individuals in their 30s and older who experience mild to moderate skin sagging. This treatment is designed to address early signs of aging by lifting and tightening the skin, making it an ideal option for those who are beginning to notice a loss of skin firmness. Generally, it is not recommended for individuals in their 20s, as their skin typically retains enough elasticity and volume that such an intervention is unnecessary. Before undergoing a PDO thread lift, a thorough consultation with a qualified practitioner is essential. This consultation will help determine if the procedure is appropriate for your specific skin condition and aesthetic goals. Factors such as the degree of sagging, skin type, and overall health will be considered to ensure optimal results and safety.

Preparing for Your PDO Thread Lift Treatment

Prior to your PDO thread lift treatment, it is essential to take a few preparatory steps to ensure the best possible outcome. On the day of your procedure, make sure to thoroughly cleanse your face, removing all make-up, creams, and lotions. This helps to create a clean surface for the treatment. In the days leading up to your appointment, there are specific substances you should avoid to minimise the risk of bruising and swelling. It is advisable to refrain from consuming alcohol and taking medications such as aspirin and ibuprofen. Additionally, steer clear of supplements like St. John's Wort, omega fish oils, Ginkgo Biloba, and vitamins C and E. These substances can thin the blood and increase the likelihood of bruising during the procedure. By following these guidelines, you can help ensure that your skin is in the best condition for your PDO thread lift, leading to optimal results and a smoother recovery process.
