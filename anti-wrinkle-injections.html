Anti-Wrinkle Injections London

Anti-wrinkle injections are the most popular treatment for reducing the signs of ageing. These injections work by relaxing facial muscles, which helps to diminish the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles. This non-invasive procedure offers a noticeable change in your appearance with minimal downtime after just one session. The primary purpose of these injections is to target fine lines and wrinkles, often the most obvious indicators of age. They are especially effective in areas where muscle activity is high, such as around the eyes and mouth. In addition to cosmetic benefits, these injections can also be used to treat migraines and excessive sweating (hyperhidrosis). Contact our dermatology clinic in London now to book a consultation if you are interested in learning more about our anti-wrinkle injection treatments.

How Long Do Anti-Wrinkle Injection Treatments Last?

The effects of anti-wrinkle injections typically last for three to four months. After this period, a follow-up treatment is necessary to maintain the desired results. With consistent treatments over the course of a year, you may notice that the frequency of required sessions decreases. Some individuals find that they only need maintenance injections every six months after regular treatments during the first year. This extended duration is due to the cumulative effects of the treatments, which help the muscles remain relaxed for longer periods.

Which Areas Can Be Treated with Anti-Wrinkle Injections?

Anti-wrinkle injections are versatile and can address various facial areas to reduce signs of ageing. The most commonly treated areas include:

Frown Lines on the Forehead:

These are vertical lines that appear between the eyebrows and can give a tired or angry look.

Crow's Feet Around the Eyes:

These are the fine lines that form at the outer corners of the eyes, often due to smiling or squinting.

Bunny Lines on the Bridge of the Nose:

These horizontal lines appear when you scrunch your nose.

Horizontal Necklace Lines:

These lines form around the neck, often due to repetitive movements and natural ageing.

Vertical Platysmal Bands:

These are vertical bands that appear on the neck, contributing to an aged appearance.

What Happens on the Day of Your Anti-Wrinkle Injections Treatment?

On the day of your treatment, one of our experienced aesthetic practitioners will walk you through the entire procedure and ensure your written consent is obtained. Together, you will determine which areas to target to achieve your desired results.

Once you are ready, the treatment will commence. Firstly, the skin in the treatment area is thoroughly cleaned. The anti-wrinkle injections are then administered into the targeted areas using a very fine, specially designed needle. These needles are crafted to minimise any discomfort. Local anaesthetic is typically not needed. While the injections might cause slight discomfort, most patients find it quite tolerable. During your 20 to 30 minute session, frown lines, forehead wrinkles, and crow's feet can all be effectively treated.

How Long Does the Treatment Take?

The treatment for anti-wrinkle injections is relatively quick and efficient, typically lasting between 20 to 30 minutes per session. During this time, our skilled practitioners administer between 10 to 15 precisely targeted injections. This ensures that the treatment is comprehensive and effectively addresses all areas of concern. Despite the number of injections, the procedure is designed to be as comfortable as possible, with minimal discomfort and downtime, allowing you to return to your daily activities immediately after your appointment.

What to Expect After Your Anti-Wrinkle Injections Treatment

Anti-wrinkle injections are a non-surgical and minimally invasive procedure, which means there is little to no downtime. However, it is recommended to avoid strenuous activities such as exercise for at least six hours following the treatment.

Most patients report only mild discomfort during the injections. Immediately after the procedure, you might experience slight swelling at the injection sites, which typically subsides within 48 hours. Some patients may also notice mild bruising, but this is usually minimal and resolves quickly. Overall, you can expect a swift recovery with minimal interruption to your daily routine, and the mild side effects should diminish within a couple of days.

Post-Treatment Skin Care

Following your anti-wrinkle injections, proper aftercare is essential for optimal results and minimal side effects. Avoid touching or rubbing the treated areas for 24 hours to ensure the injected substance remains in place. Refrain from strenuous activities, such as exercise, for at least six hours to reduce the likelihood of bruising and swelling. Keep your head upright and avoid lying down or bending over excessively for several hours post-treatment to prevent the injections from moving. Delay applying makeup for at least 24 hours to avoid irritation.

Schedule a follow-up appointment with your aesthetic practitioner at our dermatology clinic 2 to 3 weeks after the procedure. This allows for an assessment of the results and any necessary adjustments. By following these aftercare steps, you can ensure a smooth recovery and enjoy the full benefits of your treatment.

Are Anti-Wrinkle Injections Safe?

When administered by a trained medical professional, anti-wrinkle injections are generally safe and have been extensively tested. While this treatment is effective, it does carry some potential side effects, although most are mild and temporary. Common side effects include headaches and reactions at the injection site, such as redness, swelling, irritation, rash, itching, numbness, discomfort, stinging, bruising, and minor bleeding.

These reactions are usually mild to moderate, temporary, and typically occur within the first week after treatment. In rare instances, patients may experience slight drooping of the eyelid or visual disturbances. If any symptom persists for more than a week or if you have any concerns, it is crucial to contact your practitioner promptly. As with any medical procedure, it's important to weigh the risks and benefits and discuss these with your doctor to ensure the treatment is right for you.

Who Should Avoid Anti-Wrinkle Injections?

Anti-wrinkle injections are not suitable for everyone. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid these treatments due to potential risks to the baby. Individuals who have experienced adverse or allergic reactions to anti-wrinkle injections in the past should also steer clear of this procedure. Additionally, those with a current skin infection in the desired treatment area should postpone the injections until the infection has fully healed.

To determine if anti-wrinkle injections are the right choice for you, it is essential to consult with a qualified medical professional.
