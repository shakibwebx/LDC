Laser Tattoo Removal in London

Tattoo removal is the process of eliminating tattoos from the skin, typically through laser technology. People sometimes regret their tattoo decisions, finding them to be a source of discomfort, anxiety, or embarrassment. Reasons for removal can include no longer wanting an image or name associated with a past commitment or simply experiencing a change in personal style. While tattoos are generally considered permanent, if you are considering laser tattoo removal in London, our dermatology clinic offers the latest technologies to restore your skin to its pre-inked state.

Our clinic employs the cutting-edge M22 Q-Switched Nd: YAG laser, renowned for its superior technology in tattoo removal. This advanced laser emits a powerful beam that targets the tattoo ink, breaking it down into smaller particles. These particles are then absorbed and naturally eliminated by the body’s lymphatic system. Additionally, the Q-switch function allows for precise customization of the treatment based on the specific characteristics of your tattoo. This ensures a tailored and effective tattoo removal process, providing a personalised experience for each patient.

How does laser tattoo removal work?

There are different methods for removing tattoos. At the London Dermatology Centre, we use laser tattoo removal, the method considered the safest and most effective. When directed onto the skin, the laser light breaks the tattoo ink into small pieces. These smaller fragments are then taken up by the macrophages and removed from the body via the lymphatic system. As more tattoo ink particles are broken down, the appearance of the tattoo lightens and, after several sessions, the ink is no longer visible. Our experienced dermatologists ensure the process is conducted safely and effectively, tailored to each individual's needs.

M22 Q-Switched Nd: YAG Laser

We utilise the gold standard in laser tattoo removal technology: the M22 Q-Switched Nd: YAG laser. This advanced laser is specifically designed to effectively erase dark ink from the skin. The term "Q-switch" refers to the type of pulse created by the laser, producing high-energy pulses that last only a billionth of a second. This rapid pulsing concentrates the energy into powerful bursts necessary to break down tattoo particles into tiny fragments. Unlike other laser technologies, the swift action of the M22 Q-Switch prevents damage to the surrounding tissue, minimising unwanted side effects. It also significantly shatters the ink into smaller fragments, allowing for easier absorption and making it an exceptionally effective method for tattoo removal.

Q-Switch lasers are differentiated by the crystal rods inserted, which allow for variability in the wavelengths produced. In this instance, the Nd: YAG laser is used, producing 1064nm and 532nm wavelengths, providing versatility in targeting different tattoo colours. While it can address brighter colours, it is particularly effective on darker inks, such as black and blue. The Q-switched technology also enables advanced combination treatments with seven different spot sizes, ranging from 2mm to 8mm. This allows for customised treatments tailored to patients' specific tattoo removal needs, resulting in better outcomes.

How Many Sessions Will I Need?

The number of sessions required for laser tattoo removal varies for each individual because tattoos are unique. Several factors are considered when determining the necessary sessions, including your skin tone, ink type, tattoo location, size, age, colour, and the depth of ink application. These factors will be discussed during your initial consultation. Generally, most tattoos require around 2-10 sessions, with larger tattoos needing more sessions. To allow the body to heal and process the treatment, there should be at least a 6–8-week period between each session.

Can the Procedure Be Performed on Darker Skin?

The M22 Q-Switched Nd: YAG laser is effective and safe for all skin types, including darker skin. Its broad wavelength, particularly at 1064nm, targets tattoo ink without significantly affecting melanin, preventing hypopigmentation. An initial consultation ensures a personalised treatment plan. Those with darker skin may require more sessions, depending on the tattoo's size and colour, with at least 6-8 weeks of healing time between treatments. At our dermatology clinic, experienced dermatologists use top-tier equipment to ensure successful tattoo removal with minimal side effects.

What Happens Before the Procedure?

An initial consultation will take place, where our expert dermatologists will take your full medical history to determine whether you are a suitable candidate for the procedure. The tattoo will then be examined, and the recommended number of sessions will be discussed. Additionally, the practitioner will explain the procedure and the science behind the lasers used. A patch test on a small area of the tattoo is also necessary to assess for any adverse reactions that may occur.

There are also some precautions to take before your tattoo removal tretment. Avoid excessive sun exposure to the treatment area, and use a sunscreen with an SPF of at least 30 in the weeks leading up to the treatment. On the day of your appointment, make sure you have eaten, are well hydrated, and to alleviate any discomfort from the procedure, take paracetamol at least an hour before. Depending on the location of your tattoo, wear loose-fitting clothing so it does not rub against the treatment area and allows for easy access.

Is the laser tattoo removal procedure painful?

The procedure does involve some level of pain; however, it is tolerable. Patients have described feeling a heat sensation on the area while being treated and compared it to a rubber band snapping on the skin. Others have said it is equivalent to the pain felt when getting the tattoo applied. It is recommended to take paracetamol at least an hour before the procedure to alleviate any discomfort.

What happens after the tattoo removal procedure?

After your treatment, you will notice small white circles on the tattooed area. These may turn into redness and swelling, which will dissipate within a day. Within 24 hours, a thick crust will begin to form on the treated area. Do not be alarmed by this, as it is the skin’s natural response to recovery. The crust will fall off within 2 weeks. During this period, you will gradually notice the fading of the tattoo's colour and shape. Sessions are spaced 6-8 weeks apart to allow your skin to process the treatment. Results are cumulative, and completely removing the tattoo will require multiple sessions.

Will laser tattoo removal leave me with a scar?

It is very uncommon for laser tattoo removal to cause scarring. Proper aftercare can lead to faster recovery and reduce the risk of scars or infections. Before applying any antibiotic ointments, ensure the area is gently cleaned with mild soap and patted dry. You may shower a few hours after the procedure, but avoid soaking or bathing the area in a swimming pool or hot bath, as this can lead to infection and scarring. It is important to note that the risk of scarring is minimal and rare. Unlike some laser technologies, such as IPL, which target both the tattoo and the surrounding skin, the methods used at our clinic are designed to minimise scarring.

Is laser tattoo removal safe?

Unlike other tattoo removal methods, laser tattoo removal is relatively safe as it avoids damaging the skin while removing the tattoo. The M22 Q-Switch laser is not recommended for patients with inflammatory skin conditions, active infections, viral, fungal or bacterial diseases, and certain skin disorders. Side effects may include redness, swelling, and pigmentation changes. The laser beam targets only the ink, sparing the surrounding skin. In contrast, older laser technologies could potentially burn the skin.

What are the benefits of laser tattoo removal?

You will see visible results from the first session and achieve your desired outcome after a series of treatments. Following the initial treatment, you will notice a change in the colour density of the tattoo. The Q-Switch technology allows for optimal tailoring of tattoo removal based on the type of tattoo you wish to remove. Additionally, laser treatment is non-invasive (non-surgical), meaning surgical removal of the tattoo is not required, and there is reduced downtime.
