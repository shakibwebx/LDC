Sebaceous Cyst Removal in London

Sebaceous cysts are common fluid-filled small bumps that occur beneath the skin. They can arise anywhere but are usually found on the trunk of the body, neck, face, and scalp. Although they can appear in people of all ages, they are more common in teenagers and adults, sometimes causing embarrassment or discomfort. Cysts are characterised by the glands from which they arise – the sebaceous glands, which secrete the oily substance that lubricates hair and skin, are referred to as sebaceous cysts.

Sebaceous cysts can vary in size from smaller than a pea to a few centimetres across. They can slowly grow over time. In most cases, sebaceous cysts are not painful. However, if they become red, tender to touch, or painful, this can be a sign that the sebaceous cyst has become infected. Another telling sign of an infected sebaceous cyst is the presence of foul-smelling pus coming out of the cyst itself. Causes of Sebaceous Cysts

The epidermis is made up of a fine layer of skin cells that the body usually sheds, but sometimes these cells move deeper under the skin and multiply, forming a sac and causing sebaceous cysts. This abnormal development of cells may be caused by a blocked oil gland or hair follicle in the skin.

Some people are more susceptible to getting sebaceous cysts. Those who have been through puberty, are prone to acne, or have injured skin are more susceptible to getting sebaceous cysts. Sebaceous cysts are uncommon in children but occur frequently in adults. There are some rare genetic disorders, such as Gardner’s syndrome, that can also lead to the occurrence of these lumps on the skin. Thankfully, these can be quickly treated and removed. Sebaceous cysts are not contagious to others. If you need sebaceous cyst removal in London, our dermatologists are experts in treating this condition. When to Consider Sebaceous Cyst Removal

Although sebaceous cysts usually present no medical threat, removal is often sought for cosmetic reasons or to prevent complications that can arise from an inflamed or swollen cyst. These can cause pain and sometimes interfere with work, sports activities, and overall lifestyle. Some cysts grow over time, so you may want to have them removed before they worsen. If a blackhead or foul-smelling yellow liquid is present on the cyst, removal can help to avoid irritation and rupture. Although some cysts can disappear on their own, many will remain if they are not treated. With genital epidermoid cysts, discomfort, as well as pain during intercourse and urination, can be experienced. These are best removed early on before symptoms worsen.

Sebaceous cyst removal is a practical and safe choice for people who experience any form of discomfort from the skin condition. At our dermatology clinic, we offer a range of treatment options.

Since there are some rare cases in which sebaceous cysts have been known to become cancerous, our dermatologists will always perform a thorough analysis of the lumps during the consultation stage. If anything abnormal is found during the examination, a biopsy can be performed to promptly remove the cyst and prevent progression.

Treatment of Infected Sebaceous Cysts

If you have a ruptured or infected sebaceous cyst, the inflammation will need to be treated before the cyst can be removed. It is understandable to want such cysts removed quickly, especially from the face, and our dermatologists will offer a choice of therapies to achieve this swiftly, leaving your skin smooth and healthy.

Our dermatologists treat infected and swollen cysts with either steroid injections or drainage, although the latter is usually considered less effective since the cyst wall is left behind and will usually fill with fluid again. Once one of our specialists has reduced the inflammation, a minor surgical procedure can be carried out to remove the entire sebaceous cyst. If sebaceous cysts occur regularly on your skin, our dermatologists can also provide advice on skin care and preventative measures to help you avoid these blemishes in the future.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do all sebaceous cysts need to be removed?

Not all cysts require removal, but it’s often recommended if the cyst is painful, infected, growing, or cosmetically concerning. Our dermatologists will advise you based on your specific case.

What does the cyst removal procedure involve?

Cyst removal is usually a minor surgical procedure performed under local anaesthetic. The entire cyst sac is removed to help prevent recurrence.

Will the procedure leave a scar?

Some scarring is possible, but we use techniques that aim to minimise scarring wherever possible. The size and location of the cyst will influence the final appearance.

How long does the procedure take?

Most cyst removals take around 20 to 45 minutes, depending on the size and complexity. You can usually go home immediately after the procedure.

Can you remove cysts on the face or scalp?

Yes, we frequently remove cysts in visible or sensitive areas like the face, neck and scalp. Special care is taken to ensure the best cosmetic outcome.

What aftercare is needed following cyst removal?

You’ll be given detailed aftercare instructions, which may include keeping the area clean, avoiding certain activities, and attending a follow-up if stitches are used. Healing typically takes 1–2 weeks.

Is cyst removal painful?

The area is numbed with local anaesthetic, so you won’t feel pain during the procedure. You may feel mild discomfort afterwards, but this is usually manageable with over-the-counter pain relief.

Can sebaceous cysts come back after removal?

If the entire cyst sac is removed successfully, recurrence is unlikely. However, if part of the sac remains, there is a small chance it could return—which is why we aim for complete excision.
