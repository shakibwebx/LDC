IPL Skin Rejuvenation Clinic in London

IPL stands for Intense Pulsed Light and is a safe and effective treatment for rejuvenating the skin and treating conditions such as rosacea, broken thread veins, and spider naevi. IPL treatment is a type of light therapy that utilises a range of wavelengths to target the pigments in blood vessels and hairs. The energy provided by these wavelengths is absorbed by the darker pigments in blood vessels and hairs, making the IPL treatment suitable for hair reduction, thread vein removal, and rejuvenation. Skin rejuvenation is the process of reversing the signs of ageing and restoring your skin to its previous youthful appearance. Our dermatologists are experts in providing this advanced IPL laser treatment for skin rejuvenation, ensuring optimal results and healthier skin.

At our dermatology clinic in London, we offer a comprehensive range of skin rejuvenating treatments designed to restore your skin to its former glory. One of our most popular facial anti-aging treatments is the IPL skin rejuvenation treatment. This advanced procedure is highly favoured by our patients due to its gentle nature and the minimal downtime required. IPL effectively targets and treats a variety of skin concerns, such as fine lines, wrinkles, pigmentation, and uneven skin tone, helping you achieve a more youthful and radiant complexion. Our experienced dermatologists ensure that each treatment is tailored to your specific skin needs, providing you with optimal results and a rejuvenated appearance.

How does IPL work?

IPL works by reducing the signs of aging and repairing damage caused by sun exposure, trauma, illness, and other lifestyle factors. The treatment head emits a range of wavelengths that penetrate just below the skin's surface and are absorbed by melanin, the brown pigment in hair, skin, and the iris of the eye. This pigment is responsible for hair and skin color, as well as the tanning effect from UV exposure. The IPL targets and damages the melanin, prompting the body's natural repair processes to remove it, leading to a rejuvenated and more youthful appearance.

The IPL system regenerates the skin by stimulating collagen production over time. This process improves skin elasticity and texture while helping to reduce fine lines and wrinkles. At our dermatology clinic in London, we use the advanced Lumenis One IPL machine, which is equipped with a variety of treatment-specific heads and filters. This technology enables our experienced team to provide individually tailored treatments that cater to your unique skin type, ensuring optimal results and a rejuvenated appearance. The gradual increase in collagen not only enhances the skin's firmness but also helps to smooth out imperfections, giving you a more youthful and radiant complexion.

What results will I see and when will I see them?

The number of treatments you will need depends on your skin type, hair type, and desired results. The high energy levels of our laser reduce the number of treatments required to achieve rejuvenation and also make the process more cost-effective compared to extended treatments needed with lower energy lasers. Our dermatologists usually recommend a series of 4 to 6 treatments to see significant results. Each treatment should be carried out every 3 to 4 weeks, with each session lasting 10 to 20 minutes, depending on the size of the area being treated. Visible results are typically noticeable by the third session.

Is IPL safe for my skin?

When performed by a trained professional, IPL treatment is extremely safe. As with any procedure, there is a slight risk of side effects, but these are uncommon. Possible side effects include mild redness and swelling in the treated area, blistering, bruising, and changes in pigmentation such as hyperpigmentation or hypopigmentation. Typically, any redness or swelling from IPL treatment subsides within a few hours, and other side effects are very rare. Our dermatologists and aesthetic physicians conduct a comprehensive assessment and perform a patch test prior to treatment to minimise these risks.

Is IPL painful?

During the procedure, you may experience a very slight pinging sensation as the IPL treatment head is passed over your skin. Patients have described this sensation as feeling like an elastic band being flicked onto the skin. Prior to your treatment, you will undergo a patch test with one of our aesthetic practitioners. This ensures that you do not experience any adverse reactions and checks that your skin type is suitable for the IPL treatment. You will also be able to experience what the IPL treatment feels like. The IPL treatment is not painful and does not require any local anaesthetic to be applied beforehand.

What are the advantages of IPL skin rejuvenation over other treatments?

The primary advantage of IPL treatment over other rejuvenating procedures is the minimal downtime associated with it. Most patients can return to their daily activities immediately after their appointment. Occasionally, some may experience mild redness following the treatment, but this typically subsides within a few hours. Additionally, some individuals may notice slight darkening of pigmentation before it lightens.

Another significant advantage of IPL is that it does not require any anaesthetic cream beforehand, as the procedure is almost pain-free. While you may feel a slight pinging sensation during the treatment, it should not be painful. IPL treatment is also versatile, effectively addressing various skin concerns such as sun damage, rosacea, and fine lines, making it a comprehensive solution for skin rejuvenation. Overall, IPL offers a convenient, comfortable, and effective option for those seeking to improve their skin's appearance without significant downtime or discomfort.

How to care for your skin after IPL treatment

To get the best results, follow these guidelines. Keep the treated area hydrated by regularly applying a non-perfumed moisturiser with SPF 25 or higher. Avoid using harsh chemicals, including acne medications or exfoliating scrubs, for 1 to 2 days post-treatment. You can apply makeup right after the procedure. Cleanse the treated area gently using mild soap and water, and if you experience redness or irritation, pat the area dry rather than rubbing it.

Minimise exposure to the sun to avoid further damage, and steer clear of tanning beds during the treatment period. Always apply sunscreen with an SPF of 25 or higher to the treated area when exposed to sunlight. If the treated area tans, delay additional treatments for 2 weeks or until the tan fades significantly. Avoid activities that generate excessive heat or friction in the treated area, such as heavy exercise or using saunas. As long as there is no ongoing redness, blistering, or crusting, you can resume your normal activities.
