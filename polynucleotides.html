Polynucleotides Treatment London

Polynucleotides are a regenerative medicine advancement used in aesthetics, supported by extensive clinical data. They are injectable biostimulators made from chains of nucleotides acquired from Trout or Salmon DNA, which are meticulously purified. These compounds have long been utilised in hospitals for burns and wound healing. Polynucleotides play a crucial role in rejuvenation by stimulating fibroblasts, promoting collagen and elastin production, enhancing hydration, reducing pigmentation, and exhibiting antioxidant properties, thereby reducing the visible signs of ageing. The benefits of polynucleotides treatment are manifold. By improving skin elasticity and firmness, the treatment effectively reduces the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles. Enhanced hydration makes the skin look plumper and more youthful. The reduction of pigmentation leads to a clearer, more even complexion. Strong antioxidant properties protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and environmental stressors, while the regenerative properties accelerate the healing of wounds and scars, improving overall skin texture and appearance.

Benefits of Polynucleotides Treatment

  • Elevates skin quality and texture: Polynucleotides help to improve the overall texture and quality of the skin, making it smoother and more refined.
  • Boosts hydration via increased hyaluronic acid production: By stimulating the production of hyaluronic acid, polynucleotides enhance the skin's hydration levels, resulting in a plumper, more youthful appearance.
  • Diminishes the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles: The treatment promotes collagen and elastin production, which helps to reduce the visibility of fine lines and wrinkles, leading to firmer, more elastic skin.
  • Displays anti-inflammatory properties: Polynucleotides have anti-inflammatory effects that can soothe and reduce the symptoms of inflammatory skin conditions such as rosacea.
  • Addresses pigmentation and dark circles: The treatment helps to even out skin tone and reduce hyperpigmentation and dark circles, giving the complexion a clearer and more radiant look.
  • Improves the appearance of scarring: Polynucleotides aid in the healing process, improving the texture and appearance of scars from acne or other injuries.
  • Facilitates wound healing: The regenerative properties of polynucleotides accelerate wound healing, promoting faster recovery and better skin repair.
  • Addresses hair loss when applied to the scalp: When used on the scalp, polynucleotides can stimulate hair follicles, helping to combat hair loss and promote hair growth.

Overview of Polynucleotides Treatment

Polynucleotide treatments can be effectively applied to several areas, including the face (under the eyes), neck, décolletage, and scalp. To achieve optimal results, a minimum of three sessions is advised, with each session spaced approximately three weeks apart. Maintenance treatments are recommended every 6-9 months, tailored to individual needs. Patients typically begin to notice improvements within 3-4 weeks of the initial treatment.

To ensure comfort, a numbing cream is applied before the procedure. Polynucleotides integrate well with other injectable treatments such as anti-wrinkle injections, skin boosters, and dermal fillers. Unlike dermal fillers, polynucleotides do not increase volume, making them ideal for patients seeking enhanced skin quality without added fullness. Additionally, polynucleotides can prepare the skin for subsequent laser or chemical peel treatments.

Can Downtime Be Expected After Polynucleotide Treatment?

After a polynucleotide treatment, some minor bumps at the injection sites may appear, typically disappearing within 24 to 48 hours. Temporary side effects such as bruising, redness, and swelling are also possible. However, these effects are usually mild and resolve on their own within a few days. Proper aftercare is essential to ensure optimal healing. This includes keeping the treated area clean, avoiding excessive touching or rubbing, and following any specific instructions provided by your healthcare professional. Overall, downtime is minimal, allowing patients to quickly return to their daily activities.

Who is Suitable for Polynucleotide Treatment?

Polynucleotide treatment is suitable for all skin types and for those who want to improve their skin quality and hydration. However, this treatment is not suitable for pregnant or breastfeeding individuals, those with a history of autoimmune diseases, active skin infections, fish allergies (which may require patch testing), or hypersensitivity/allergy to polynucleotide products. A thorough medical history review is conducted during the consultation at our dermatology clinic in London to ensure the treatment is safe and appropriate for each patient.
